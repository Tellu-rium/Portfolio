This famous quote by my favourite Jiddu Krishnamurthy had me thinking about another concept, "consciousness vs reality". Let's deep dive into what this means.

Let's assume you are holding a blue paint. Now, since you were taught in school that this type of colour, which is "bluish" in shade, is called blue. To know it's blue, you should know what blue means. Can you see a cycle forming? Now, what if I say it's nothing but a light of wavelength 450 nm hitting your eyes? Now, when you see this paint, your brain interprets it as the 450nm wavelength, which is blue. So this is the illusion of perception. What you observe is not being observed; it's you, your consciousness, that's being observed. The observer (you) is projecting their own conditioned thoughts onto the object. In that moment, what you are really observing is the content of your own consciousness. You aren't seeing the paint; you are seeing your own mind reacting to the paint. A human being, or any living being capable of thinking or using their consciousness, will create a scenario in their brain that leads to a conclusion. The observer is not separate from those thoughts or that consciousness; he's a part of them, they are his.
